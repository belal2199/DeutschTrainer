TOPIC 01 — GROCERY SHOPPING (Einkaufen im Supermarkt)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
der Einkaufswagen     — shopping cart
die Kasse             — checkout / cash register
der Kassenbon         — receipt
das Sonderangebot     — special offer / deal
der Aufschnitt        — cold cuts / deli meats
die Tiefkühlkost      — frozen food
das Pfand             — deposit (on bottles)
die Tüte              — bag (plastic/paper)
der Eigenmarke        — store brand / own brand
das Mindesthaltbarkeitsdatum (MHD) — best before date

--- VERBS ---
einkaufen             — to go shopping / to buy groceries
abwiegen              — to weigh (e.g. fruits and vegetables)
scannen               — to scan (at self-checkout)
zurückgeben           — to return (a product)
einlösen              — to redeem (a coupon / Pfand)

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Haben Sie eine Kundenkarte?"
  → Do you have a loyalty card?
"Möchten Sie eine Tüte?"
  → Would you like a bag?
"Das macht zusammen 14 Euro 60."
  → That comes to 14 euros 60.
"Haben Sie es kleiner?"
  → Do you have anything smaller? (smaller change)
"Die Waage ist dort drüben."
  → The scale is over there.
"Das ist leider ausverkauft."
  → That's unfortunately sold out.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Wo finde ich die Milchprodukte bitte?"
  → Where can I find the dairy products?
"Ich würde gerne das Pfand zurückgeben."
  → I'd like to return the bottles for the deposit.
"Ist das im Angebot diese Woche?"
  → Is this on offer this week?
"Können Sie mir das einpacken?"
  → Can you pack that for me?
"Ich glaube, das wurde zweimal gescannt."
  → I think that was scanned twice.
"Gibt es hier eine Selbstkasse?"
  → Is there a self-checkout here?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• Locals say "Aldi" like a religion — "Ich geh kurz zu Aldi" is a full sentence
  and a social event in the Ruhr area.
• "Dat" instead of "das": "Dat kostet zu viel, Alter."
• "Wat" instead of "was": "Wat soll dat kosten?"
• "Ne?" at the end for confirmation: "Is echt günstig hier, ne?"
• At the Markt (outdoor market) in Duisburg-Marxloh or the Wochenmarkt,
  vendors often greet you with "Na, wat darf's sein?"
• Ruhr shortcut: "Ich gönn mir dat" = I'll treat myself to that.

--- PRO TIP ---
In German supermarkets (REWE, Edeka, Aldi, Lidl), baggers do NOT exist —
you pack your own groceries at high speed. Don't panic: it's totally normal
to put things back in your cart first and bag them at the side table.
Nobody judges you. Also: always bring your own bag — buying one every time
feels wasteful and locals notice.

TOPIC 02 — TABLE TENNIS TRAINING (Tischtennistraining)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
der Schläger          — paddle / bat
der Aufschlag         — serve
der Rückhand          — backhand
die Vorhand           — forehand
der Topspin           — topspin
der Schnitt           — slice / cut (spin)
das Netz              — net
der Satz              — set
die Trainingshalle    — training hall
der Vereinskollege    — club teammate

--- VERBS ---
aufschlagen           — to serve
returnieren           — to return (the ball)
trainieren            — to train / to practice
mitspielen            — to join / play along
einwärmen             — to warm up
üben                  — to practice
verlieren / gewinnen  — to lose / to win

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Du bist dran mit Aufschlag."
  → It's your serve.
"Noch mal — das war aus."
  → Again — that was out.
"Willst du eine Runde mitspielen?"
  → Do you want to join for a round?
"Wir spielen auf zwei Gewinnsätze."
  → We're playing best of three.
"Dein Rückhand ist echt stark geworden!"
  → Your backhand has really gotten strong!
"Heute machen wir nur Grundlagen."
  → Today we're just working on the basics.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Kannst du mir zeigen, wie man den Topspin macht?"
  → Can you show me how to do the topspin?
"Ich bin noch Anfänger, aber ich lerne schnell."
  → I'm still a beginner, but I learn fast.
"Spielen wir noch einen Satz?"
  → Shall we play one more set?
"War das drin oder draußen?"
  → Was that in or out?
"Ich muss noch an meinem Aufschlag arbeiten."
  → I still need to work on my serve.
"Kann ich den Schläger kurz ausprobieren?"
  → Can I try that paddle for a sec?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• "Alter, der Ball war drin!" — very common informal protest at Ruhr sports clubs.
• "Mach mal Dampf" = pick up the pace / get moving — used when someone's playing slow.
• "Kumpel" is the classic Ruhr word for buddy/mate — comes from mining culture
  (Bergbau). Using it at the club earns instant warmth: "Komm, Kumpel, noch'n Satz!"
• "Ey, das war Glück!" = "Ey, that was lucky!" — said with a grin, never offensively.
• At Ruhr sports clubs, Vereinsabend (club evening) often matters as much as training.
  Show up for both.

--- PRO TIP ---
German sports clubs (Vereine) have a strong social culture — the training is real,
but so is the chat afterwards. Saying "Ich bin beim Verein" (I'm in the club) carries
genuine social weight here. If someone invites you to stay after practice for a
Kaltgetränk (cold drink), say yes. That's where real friendships are built.

TOPIC 03 — AT THE DOCTOR'S (Beim Arzt)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
die Krankenversicherungskarte  — health insurance card
der Termin                     — appointment
das Wartezimmer                — waiting room
die Überweisung                — referral (to a specialist)
der Facharzt / die Fachärztin  — specialist doctor
die Bescheinigung              — certificate / written note
das Rezept                     — prescription
die Praxis                     — doctor's office / practice
die Sprechstunde               — office hours / consultation hours
der Hausarzt / die Hausärztin  — general practitioner (GP)

--- VERBS ---
untersuchen           — to examine
überweisen            — to refer (to a specialist)
verschreiben          — to prescribe
eintragen             — to register / enter your details
krank schreiben       — to sign off sick (give sick note)
abholen               — to pick up (a prescription / results)

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Haben Sie Ihre Versichertenkarte dabei?"
  → Do you have your insurance card with you?
"Sind Sie zum ersten Mal bei uns?"
  → Is this your first visit with us?
"Der Arzt hat gleich Zeit für Sie."
  → The doctor will see you shortly.
"Wo genau haben Sie die Schmerzen?"
  → Where exactly does it hurt?
"Ich stelle Ihnen eine Überweisung aus."
  → I'll write you a referral.
"Sie bekommen eine Krankschreibung für drei Tage."
  → You'll get a sick note for three days.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Ich hätte gerne einen Termin, bitte."
  → I'd like to make an appointment, please.
"Ich habe seit zwei Tagen Halsschmerzen und leichtes Fieber."
  → I've had a sore throat and a slight fever for two days.
"Ich nehme bereits Medikamente — ist das ein Problem?"
  → I'm already taking medication — is that a problem?
"Können Sie das bitte aufschreiben?"
  → Could you write that down for me, please?
"Ich verstehe das nicht ganz — können Sie das nochmal erklären?"
  → I don't quite understand — could you explain that again?
"Brauche ich dafür ein Rezept?"
  → Do I need a prescription for that?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• "Ich bin beim Doktor" — locals drop the article informally.
• In Duisburg, many Hausärzte have very diverse patient bases — don't be surprised
  if the receptionist switches to easier German or even English if they sense a language barrier.
  You can say: "Ich lerne noch Deutsch — bitte langsam." (I'm still learning German — please speak slowly.)
• "Et tut weh" (Ruhrdeutsch) = "Es tut weh" — it hurts.
• Locals often say "Ich bin krankgeschrieben" very casually when calling in sick at work.

--- PRO TIP ---
In Germany, you MUST register with a Hausarzt first — they are your gateway to everything.
Without a referral (Überweisung), seeing a specialist is harder and often more expensive.
Register with a Hausarzt near you as soon as possible, even before you're sick.
Call in the morning: most practices release same-day appointments right when they open.

TOPIC 04 — TAKING THE BUS & TRAM (Bus und Straßenbahn fahren)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
die Haltestelle       — stop (bus/tram)
der Fahrschein        — ticket
das Ticket            — ticket (colloquial)
die Linie             — line (e.g. Linie 901)
der Anschluss         — connection (to another line)
die Verspätung        — delay
der Kontrolleur       — ticket inspector
das Monatsticket      — monthly pass
die Endhaltestelle    — final stop / terminus
der Nahverkehr        — local public transport

--- VERBS ---
einsteigen            — to board / get on
aussteigen            — to get off
umsteigen             — to change (lines)
verpassen             — to miss (a bus/tram)
entwerten             — to validate (a ticket — older machines)
checken / einchecken  — to check in (newer systems)

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Bitte Fahrscheine bereithalten!"
  → Please have your tickets ready!
"Nächste Haltestelle: Duisburg Hauptbahnhof."
  → Next stop: Duisburg Central Station.
"Diese Linie fährt nur bis Mühlheim — bitte umsteigen."
  → This line only goes to Mülheim — please change trains.
"Ihr Ticket ist leider nicht gültig."
  → Your ticket is unfortunately not valid.
"Der Bus hat circa 10 Minuten Verspätung."
  → The bus is approximately 10 minutes late.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Fährt diese Linie zum Hauptbahnhof?"
  → Does this line go to the main station?
"Wo muss ich umsteigen, um zur Königstraße zu kommen?"
  → Where do I need to change to get to Königstraße?
"Ich habe den Bus verpasst — wann kommt der nächste?"
  → I missed the bus — when is the next one?
"Kann ich das Ticket auch an der Haltestelle kaufen?"
  → Can I buy the ticket at the stop too?
"Entschuldigung, ist das hier die Richtung Innenstadt?"
  → Excuse me, is this the direction towards the city centre?
"Können Sie mir Bescheid geben, wenn wir an der Stadtmitte sind?"
  → Could you let me know when we reach the city centre?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• The VRR (Verkehrsverbund Rhein-Ruhr) is the transport network — you'll hear "VRR-Ticket"
  and "Deutschlandticket" constantly. The Deutschlandticket (49€/month) covers ALL
  local transport in Germany — get it immediately.
• "Die Bahn is wieder spät" (said with resigned acceptance) — a near-universal Ruhr expression.
• "Ich nehm die 901" — locals refer to lines with "die" + number. The 901 Straßenbahn
  in Duisburg is iconic.
• "Ey, fährt der hier noch?" = "Does this one still stop here?" — classic informal question.
• Ruhr pronunciation: "Ich steig hier aus" often sounds like "Ich steig hia aus."

--- PRO TIP ---
Get the Deutschlandticket (currently ~49€/month) — it covers all buses, trams, and regional
trains across Germany. For Duisburg: the VRR app shows real-time departures and you can
buy tickets digitally. Always have a valid ticket — inspectors (Kontrolleure) work plainclothes
and the fine is 60€ on the spot, no exceptions and no sympathy.

TOPIC 05 — AT THE PHARMACY (In der Apotheke)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
das Medikament        — medication
das Rezept            — prescription
das Schmerzmittel     — painkiller
das Antibiotikum      — antibiotic
die Salbe             — ointment / cream
der Hustensaft        — cough syrup
die Nebenwirkung      — side effect
die Packungsbeilage   — package insert / leaflet
das Zuzahlung         — co-payment
die Apothekerin       — (female) pharmacist

--- VERBS ---
einlösen              — to redeem (a prescription)
empfehlen             — to recommend
einnehmen             — to take (medication)
vertragen             — to tolerate (a medication)
verschreiben          — to prescribe

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Haben Sie ein Rezept dafür?"
  → Do you have a prescription for that?
"Das müssen Sie dreimal täglich nach dem Essen einnehmen."
  → You need to take this three times a day after eating.
"Das ist rezeptfrei — das können Sie direkt kaufen."
  → That's available without a prescription — you can buy it directly.
"Haben Sie Allergien auf bestimmte Wirkstoffe?"
  → Do you have any allergies to specific active ingredients?
"Das kostet mit Kassenrezept nur die Zuzahlung — 5 Euro."
  → With an insurance prescription, you only pay the co-payment — 5 euros.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Ich habe ein Rezept von meinem Arzt."
  → I have a prescription from my doctor.
"Was empfehlen Sie gegen Kopfschmerzen?"
  → What do you recommend for headaches?
"Ich bin gegen Penizillin allergisch."
  → I'm allergic to penicillin.
"Gibt es das auch als Generikum?"
  → Is there a generic version of that?
"Kann ich das auch während der Schwangerschaft nehmen?"
  → Can this also be taken during pregnancy?
"Haben Sie etwas Natürliches gegen Erkältung?"
  → Do you have something natural for a cold?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• Germans trust their Apotheke enormously — it's not just a shop, it's advisory.
  Don't be shy about describing symptoms in detail.
• "Haben Sie wat gegen...?" — Ruhrdeutsch version of "Do you have something for...?"
• Locals often say "Ich geh zur Apotheke" even for minor things — totally normal.
• Duisburg tip: many Apotheken in areas like Marxloh have Turkish or Arabic-speaking staff.
  You can ask: "Spricht hier jemand Arabisch?" if needed.

--- PRO TIP ---
In Germany, Aspirin, Ibuprofen, and most common meds are ONLY sold in pharmacies —
NOT in supermarkets. You can get smaller packs at a Drogerie (DM, Rossmann) but proper
medication always goes through the Apotheke. Pharmacists are highly trained and give real
medical advice — use them before booking a doctor for minor issues.

TOPIC 06 — AT THE BAKERY (In der Bäckerei)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
das Brötchen          — bread roll
der Vollkornbrot      — wholegrain bread
das Croissant         — croissant
die Brezel            — pretzel
der Aufschnitt        — cold cuts (for sandwiches)
die Bäckereiverkäuferin — bakery saleswoman
die Scheibe           — slice
die Torte             — cake / gateau
der Kaffee zum Mitnehmen — coffee to go
das Tablett           — tray

--- VERBS ---
aufschneiden          — to slice
belegen               — to top / fill (a sandwich)
bestellen             — to order
einpacken             — to wrap / pack
vorbestellen          — to pre-order (e.g. for Sunday)

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Was darf es sein?"
  → What can I get you? (most common opener)
"Soll ich das aufschneiden?"
  → Should I slice it?
"Wir haben leider kein Roggenbrot mehr."
  → We unfortunately don't have rye bread left.
"Möchten Sie das einpacken oder hier essen?"
  → Would you like it wrapped or eating here?
"Heute frisch gebacken!"
  → Baked fresh today!

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Vier Brötchen bitte — zwei Körner und zwei normale."
  → Four bread rolls please — two seeded and two plain.
"Einmal Schwarzbrot, bitte in Scheiben geschnitten."
  → One dark bread, please sliced.
"Haben Sie auch belegte Brötchen?"
  → Do you also have filled rolls?
"Was empfehlen Sie heute?"
  → What do you recommend today?
"Kann ich ein Brot für morgen vorbestellen?"
  → Can I pre-order a loaf for tomorrow?
"Einen Kaffee bitte — mit Milch."
  → One coffee please — with milk.

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• "Schrippen" — what some older Berliners call bread rolls, but in the Ruhr it's
  always "Brötchen." Don't let anyone tell you otherwise.
• "Einmal Mettbrötchen bitte!" — raw minced pork on a bread roll with onions.
  It's a Ruhr delicacy and people eat it for breakfast. Yes, raw. Yes, it's delicious.
• "Wat kostet dat?" instead of "Was kostet das?" — totally fine at a local Bäckerei.
• Many Bäckereien in NRW open at 6:00 AM — going for fresh Brötchen on Sunday morning
  is practically a ritual.

--- PRO TIP ---
Germans are extremely serious about their Brot — Germany has over 3,000 registered
bread varieties. Saying "Das schmeckt wirklich gut!" (That tastes really good!) to a
baker is a fast-track to a warm relationship and sometimes a free sample. Sunday morning
Brötchen runs are a cultural event — join them.

TOPIC 07 — AT THE POST OFFICE (Auf der Post / DHL-Filiale)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
das Paket             — parcel / package
der Brief             — letter
die Briefmarke        — stamp
das Einschreiben      — registered mail
der Absender          — sender
der Empfänger         — recipient
die Sendungsnummer    — tracking number
der Paketschein       — shipping label
die Packstation       — DHL parcel locker (automated)
die Nachnahme         — cash on delivery

--- VERBS ---
aufgeben              — to send / submit (a package)
verfolgen             — to track (a parcel)
abholen               — to pick up
frankieren            — to affix postage / stamp
weiterleiten          — to forward (mail)

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Darf ich den Inhalt erfragen?"
  → May I ask about the contents?
"Möchten Sie das versichern lassen?"
  → Would you like to insure it?
"Hier ist Ihre Sendungsnummer."
  → Here is your tracking number.
"Das dauert zwei bis drei Werktage."
  → That takes two to three working days.
"Sie können das Paket auch an einer Packstation abgeben."
  → You can also drop off the package at a parcel locker.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Ich möchte dieses Paket nach Marokko schicken."
  → I'd like to send this package to Morocco.
"Was kostet ein Brief nach Frankreich?"
  → How much does a letter to France cost?
"Wie lange dauert die Lieferung?"
  → How long does delivery take?
"Ich habe eine Benachrichtigung bekommen — mein Paket liegt hier?"
  → I received a notification — is my package here?
"Kann ich das als Einschreiben schicken?"
  → Can I send this as registered mail?
"Wo ist die nächste Packstation?"
  → Where is the nearest parcel locker?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• DHL is dominant in Germany — "Ich schick dat per DHL" is the standard phrase.
• Packstationen are everywhere in Duisburg — learn to use the DHL app and you'll
  almost never need to queue at the Post again.
• "Der Postbote war schon da" (The postman already came) — said with mild frustration
  when you missed a delivery.
• "Ich hab 'ne Karte gekriegt" = I got a (missed delivery) card — very common
  because Germans are often not home during delivery hours.

--- PRO TIP ---
Set up a DHL Packstation account immediately. Most Germans have one. When you're not
home for a delivery, packages go there automatically if you enable it in the app.
This saves you countless trips to the post office. Also: Deutsche Post and DHL are
the same company — one does letters, one does parcels.

TOPIC 08 — AT THE BANK (Bei der Bank / Sparkasse)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
das Girokonto         — current account / checking account
die Kontonummer       — account number
die IBAN              — IBAN
die Überweisung       — bank transfer
der Dauerauftrag      — standing order
die Lastschrift       — direct debit
die Geheimzahl (PIN)  — PIN
der Geldautomat       — ATM
die Filiale           — branch
das Kreditinstitut    — financial institution

--- VERBS ---
überweisen            — to transfer (money)
einzahlen             — to deposit
abheben               — to withdraw
eröffnen              — to open (an account)
kündigen              — to cancel / close (an account)
einrichten            — to set up (a standing order)

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Haben Sie einen Termin?"
  → Do you have an appointment?
"Darf ich Ihren Ausweis sehen?"
  → May I see your ID?
"Die Kontoführungsgebühr beträgt monatlich 5 Euro."
  → The account maintenance fee is 5 euros per month.
"Wir schicken Ihnen die Karte per Post."
  → We'll send the card to you by post.
"Brauchen Sie Hilfe mit dem Online-Banking?"
  → Do you need help with online banking?

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Ich möchte ein Girokonto eröffnen."
  → I'd like to open a current account.
"Welche Unterlagen brauche ich dafür?"
  → What documents do I need for that?
"Kann ich einen Dauerauftrag einrichten?"
  → Can I set up a standing order?
"Ich brauche meine IBAN für eine Überweisung."
  → I need my IBAN for a transfer.
"Der Geldautomat hat meine Karte einbehalten."
  → The ATM kept my card.
"Gibt es hier eine Bearbeitungsgebühr?"
  → Is there a processing fee here?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• Sparkasse Duisburg is THE local institution — older residents are very loyal to it.
  Saying "Ich bin bei der Sparkasse" is like saying you're a local.
• For younger people and newcomers: N26, DKB (Deutsche Kreditbank), and ING are
  popular online banks — no fees, full English apps.
• "Ich muss kurz Kohle ziehen" = I need to quickly withdraw cash (Kohle = Ruhr slang for money,
  literally "coal" — a nod to the mining history).
• Cash is still king in Germany. "Nur bar" (cash only) signs are common even in 2026.

--- PRO TIP ---
Open your account as soon as you arrive — you need a Wohnsitzanmeldung
(registration of residence) first. Take your passport, Anmeldebescheinigung,
and a proof of address. Without a German bank account you cannot receive a salary,
pay rent by Überweisung, or set up most contracts. It's the most important admin
task after registering your address.

TOPIC 09 — AT THE GYM (Im Fitnessstudio)
================================================================================

--- NOUNS ---
das Mitglied          — member
der Vertrag           — contract / membership agreement
die Mitgliedschaft    — membership
das Freihantelbereich — free weights area
das Laufband          — treadmill
der Kursplan          — class schedule
der Spind             — locker
das Handtuch          — towel
die Einheit           — session / unit
der Personaltrainer   — personal trainer

--- VERBS ---
anmelden              — to register / sign up
trainieren            — to train / work out
dehnen                — to stretch
absagen               — to cancel (a class)
buchen                — to book (a class)
kündigen              — to cancel (a membership)

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Möchten Sie eine Probetraining machen?"
  → Would you like to do a trial workout?
"Bitte wischen Sie die Geräte nach der Benutzung ab."
  → Please wipe down the equipment after use.
"Der Kurs ist leider ausgebucht."
  → The class is unfortunately fully booked.
"Die Mindestlaufzeit beträgt 12 Monate."
  → The minimum term is 12 months.
"Sie brauchen Hallenschuhe für den Kursraum."
  → You need indoor shoes for the studio room.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Ich würde gerne eine Probeeinheit machen."
  → I'd like to do a trial session.
"Was kostet die Mitgliedschaft im Monat?"
  → What does the membership cost per month?
"Gibt es eine Einführung in die Geräte?"
  → Is there an introduction to the equipment?
"Kann ich den Vertrag monatlich kündigen?"
  → Can I cancel the contract on a monthly basis?
"Wie lange läuft die Mindestlaufzeit?"
  → How long is the minimum contract term?
"Haben Sie Kurse für Anfänger?"
  → Do you have classes for beginners?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• McFit, FitX, und RSG Group studios are all over the Ruhr — cheap, no-frills, busy.
• "Ich bin am Pumpen" = I'm lifting / working out (pumpen = pump, very casual gym slang).
• "Alter, wieviel machste?" = "Mate, how much are you lifting?" — common gym floor chat.
• Ruhr gym culture tends to be direct and friendly. Nodding at regulars is standard.
  Don't be surprised if someone gives you unsolicited training advice — it's meant kindly.

--- PRO TIP ---
German gym contracts are notoriously hard to cancel — the Kündigungsfrist (notice period)
is usually 3 months before the contract ends and it must be in writing (Kündigung per Brief
or email with Lesebestätigung). Set a calendar reminder. Missing it means another full year
of payments. Some gyms require certified mail (Einschreiben) — always read the contract
Paragraph by Paragraph before signing.

TOPIC 10 — AT A RESTAURANT / IMBISS (Im Restaurant / am Imbiss)
================================================================================

das Trinkgeld         — tip

--- VERBS ---

empfehlen             — to recommend
reservieren           — to reserve
aufteilen             — to split (the bill)
bezahlen              — to pay

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---

  → Have you decided yet?

zurückgeben           — to return
verlängern            — to extend (a loan)
suchen                — to search
vormerken             — to reserve / put on hold
stöbern               — to browse

--- SENTENCES YOU WILL HEAR ---
"Haben Sie schon einen Bibliotheksausweis?"
  → Do you already have a library card?
"Die Ausleihdauer beträgt vier Wochen."
  → The borrowing period is four weeks.
"Das Buch ist gerade ausgeliehen — soll ich es für Sie vormerken?"
  → That book is currently borrowed — shall I reserve it for you?
"Sie können auch online verlängern."
  → You can also renew online.
"Die Rückgabe ist hier vorne."
  → Returns are here at the front.

--- SENTENCES YOU CAN SAY ---
"Ich würde gerne einen Bibliotheksausweis beantragen."
  → I'd like to apply for a library card.
"Haben Sie auch Bücher auf Arabisch / Türkisch / Englisch?"
  → Do you also have books in Arabic / Turkish / English?
"Ich suche Bücher zum Deutschlernen."
  → I'm looking for books for learning German.
"Gibt es hier WLAN?"
  → Is there Wi-Fi here?
"Kann ich hier auch am Computer arbeiten?"
  → Can I also work on a computer here?
"Bis wann muss ich das zurückgeben?"
  → By when do I have to return this?

--- RUHRGEBIET FLAVOR ---
• The Duisburg Stadtbibliothek (Steinsche Gasse) is a great resource and often
  underused by newcomers. It's free to join with your Anmeldebescheinigung.
• Many branches offer multilingual collections — ask for the "fremdsprachige Medien" section.
• "Ich geh kurz in die Bücherei" — note: "Bücherei" and "Bibliothek" are used
  interchangeably in the Ruhr.
• Onleihe (digital lending) lets you borrow e-books and audiobooks from home with your
  library card — massive for German learners.

--- PRO TIP ---
A German library card is free or very cheap, but it is one of the most
underrated tools for a newcomer. You get: free books, free magazines, free digital
learning resources (including Babbel and Rosetta Stone at some branches), free Wi-Fi,
a quiet workspace, and a place where staff genuinely want to help you. Register in your
first week. It's the most civilised thing you can do.

STANDARD GERMAN → RUHRDEUTSCH
das             → dat         ("Dat ist gut.")
was             → wat         ("Wat kostet dat?")
es              → et          ("Et geht los!")
ich habe        → ich hab     ("Ich hab keine Ahnung.")
kannst du       → kannste     ("Kannste mir helfen?")
hast du         → hasse       ("Hasse wat gesagt?")
ein(en)         → nen         ("Gib mir nen Tipp.")
ist es          → isses       ("Isses weit?")

FILLER WORDS & EXPRESSIONS (very Ruhr)
"Alter!"        → Mate! / Dude! (neutral, used constantly)
"Ey!"           → Hey! (attention-getter, not aggressive)
"Ne?"           → Right? / Yeah? (confirmation at end of sentence)
"Wa?"           → Right? / Huh? (also confirmation, more casual)
"Und so"        → And stuff / and so on
"Klar, Kumpel!" → Sure, mate! (Kumpel = buddy, from mining heritage)
"Mach mal!"     → Go on then! / Do it!
"Lass mal!"     → Let's go! / Come on!
"Ich gönn mir dat" → I'll treat myself to that

PRONUNCIATION NOTES
• The "r" at the end of words is often swallowed: "Wasser" sounds like "Wassa"
• "ig" at the end sounds like "ich" in standard German, 
  but Ruhr speakers often say it as "ick": "richtig" → "richtick"
• "ei" can sound more like "ee" in fast Ruhr speech
• Rising intonation at end of statements (sounds like a question) — this is normal!
